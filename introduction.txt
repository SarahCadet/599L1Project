--------------------------------------------------

Mini scene:
	k-CNF formula:
	(clause) and (clause) and (clause)
	(x1 or x3 or x4) and (x2 or x1 or x3) 
	(x1 or not x3 or x4) and (not x2 or x1 or x3) 

Voiceover:
	(Gradually read as text and animation are shown) For k-SAT we need to know what a k-CNF equation is. A k-CNF equation is a conjunction of clauses, where each clause has k terms that are orred together. And each term is a boolean variable that can be negated. 


Main Scene:
	k-SAT:  Given a k-CNF formula with n variables and m clauses, decide whether it is satisfiable. 
	x1 = False
	x2 = False
	x3 = False
	x4 = False

	So going back to our equation (x1 or not x3 or x4) and (not x2 or x1 or x3) 
	Plug into: (True or not False or False) and (not False or True or True) 
	(False or True or False) and (True or False or False) 
	(True) and (True) 

Voicover:
(Read Definition). Here satisfiability refers to determine whether or not we can assign a True/False value to each variable so that after evaluating the equation with these assignments, it evaluates to True
Going back to our example from before. One satisfiable assignment would be setting all variables to False. Plugging in these values, we can see that the equation then evaluates to True
